Proposed Solutions

Based on our research, a natural way to lessen the effects of typhoons is to prioritize the restoration of mangrove forests. These ecosystems serve as natural barriers, reduced flooding and community protectors.

Rhovie Baysa

Spreading awareness before the typhoon: Organize presentations or information booths at school to educate your classmates and community members about typhoons. You can create posters, flyers, or short presentations highlighting typhoon preparedness tips like evacuation routes, emergency supplies, and basic first aid.

Mariel Francheska Samson

Volunteer with groups that get communities ready for typhoons (after them too!). Plant trees, especially mangroves (they shield the coast from strong waves). Join clean-up drives to help rebuild after a storm.

Elijah James Del Rosario
