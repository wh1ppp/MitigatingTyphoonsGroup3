A typhoon is a large, powerful and violent tropical cyclone. It is a low pressure area rotating counter-clockwise and containing rising warm air that forms over warm water in the Western Pacific Ocean.

Typhoons can inflict terrible damage due to thunderstorms, violent winds, torrential rain, floods, landslides, large and very big waves associated with storm surges.

Classifications of Typhoons.
Tropical Depression – maximum winds from 35 kph to 63 kph
Tropical Storm – maximum winds from 64 kph to 118 kph
Typhoons – maximum winds exceeding 118 kph
