The Philippines is vulnerable to many natural
disasters due to it's geographical location as well
as it's physical environment. The country is located
along the route of turbulent and damaging
cyclones in the Pacific, as well as the "Ring of Fire."
Given the circumstances, negative consequences,
not just on the lives and attributes of the Filipino
people, but additionally impact the country's economy,
as disaster effects could lead to widespread harm to
the environment and economy.

If they aren't anticipated and prepared for, natural disasters have the potential to endanger lives, property, and financial resources.

These are frequently accompanied by strong winds, torrential rains, floods, storm surges, and mudslides/landslides.
