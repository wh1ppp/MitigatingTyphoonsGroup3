A. What is the basic information of the typhoon (This includes the wind speed, the amount of precipitation it brought, when and where it occurred, etc.) 4.28.2024

In the Philippines, super typhoon Koppu's extremely heavy rainfall has resulted in deadly flooding and mudslides. landed around 360 kilometers northeast of Manila, the capital of the Philippines, over the town of Casiguran in the Aurora province. When Koppu made landfall, it was a category 3 typhoon with sustained winds of up to 185 km/h and gusts of up to 220 km/h. Over the course of five days, the slow-moving typhoon also dumped 300–760 mm of rain on central and northern Luzon. At its peak, it reached 10 minute sustained speeds of 185 km/h. It entered the PAR (Philippine Area of Responsibility). The typhoon was ‘born’ on October 13, 2015, and died down on October 21, 2015. Additionally, shelters were damaged by the typhoon, particularly along the Philippines' eastern coast.

B. What is the basic information of the region that has been greatly affected by the typhoon (This includes the geographical location, economic status, population, etc.)? 4.28.2024

48 people lost their lives, 83 were injured, and more than 3.13 million people, 626,000 families were impacted by Typhoon Koppu. Over 138,000 homes were damaged, with 19,000 completely demolished, according to the National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC). The council also stated that livestock and agricultural products valued at about 9 billion Philippine Pesos (PHP) were lost. Nearly all of the infrastructure and structures in Casiguran, Aurora province, where the typhoon made landfall, were damaged, according to Rappler. Dinalungan and Dilasag, two more nearby settlements, were cut off from the outside world, according to officials.
